================================================================================
VOLTAGE FINGERPRINT ANALYSIS — 100 cm² MEA CO2RR
Effect of Flow-Field Configuration on Cell Voltage Behavior During FE(CO) Decline
================================================================================

System: AgNPs GDE (>100nm, 0.4–0.5 mg/cm²) | Sustanion AEM | IrO2-TiPt anode
Operation: 50 mM CH3COOH humidified CO2 (200 ml/min) | 100 ml/min 0.1M KHCO3 | 100 mA/cm²
Date: 2026-05-04


================================================================================
1. CORE PRINCIPLE: CELL VOLTAGE AS A FAILURE FINGERPRINT
================================================================================

In galvanostatic (constant current) operation, cell voltage encodes the
electrolyzer's operating state:

  V_cell = E_rev + η_act(cathode) + η_act(anode) + iR + η_conc(cathode) + η_conc(anode)

When selectivity shifts from CO2RR→CO to HER, what happens to each term
determines whether voltage goes UP, DOWN, or STAYS FLAT.

The three flow-field configurations each trigger a DIFFERENT failure mechanism.
That mechanism leaves a distinct, reproducible voltage fingerprint — independent
of what the FE(CO) trace looks like.

  Observed results (100 cm² MEA):
    • Single-serpentine:  55 h durability — voltage DECREASES with FE drop
    • Quad-serpentine:    ~43 h durability — voltage DECREASES with FE drop
    • Parallel:           ~20 h durability — voltage STABLE despite FE drop

  Contrast (Ni-SAC cathode system, separate experiment):
    • Ni-SAC:             voltage INCREASES proportionally with FE(H2) rise


================================================================================
2. SERPENTINE FLOW-FIELDS (SINGLE AND QUAD): VOLTAGE DECREASES
================================================================================

ROOT FAILURE MECHANISM:
  Salt crystal formation at CO2 inlet → CO2 starvation downstream →
  GDE back-flooding by product water

SEQUENCE OF EVENTS:
  1. K2CO3 / KHCO3 salt nucleates preferentially at the serpentine CO2 inlet
     (highest OH⁻ production → most CO3²⁻ → highest K⁺ saturation)
  2. Salt partially blocks the inlet channel → CO2 flow downstream restricted
  3. CO2 is no longer sweeping product water out of the GDE pores
  4. Product water invades GDE pores from the catalyst-layer side (back-flooding)
  5. GDE transitions from gas-phase-accessible to liquid-saturated electrode
  6. HER takes over (water is the new reactant source)

IMPACT ON EACH VOLTAGE TERM:

  ┌─────────────────────────────┬───────────┬────────────────────────────────────┐
  │ Voltage term                │ Direction │ Mechanism                          │
  ├─────────────────────────────┼───────────┼────────────────────────────────────┤
  │ E_rev                       │ ↓ −0.11V  │ E°(CO2→CO) ≈ −0.52V vs RHE        │
  │                             │           │ E°(HER)    ≈ −0.41V vs RHE         │
  │                             │           │ HER thermodynamically cheaper       │
  ├─────────────────────────────┼───────────┼────────────────────────────────────┤
  │ η_conc(CO2)                 │ ↓ Large   │ CO2 blocked → CO2 concentration    │
  │                             │           │ overpotential disappears entirely   │
  │                             │           │ HER from water has no CO2 barrier  │
  ├─────────────────────────────┼───────────┼────────────────────────────────────┤
  │ iR (ohmic)                  │ ↓ Signif. │ Flooded GDE pores create continuous│
  │                             │           │ ionic path to AEM. Dry GDE-AEM     │
  │                             │           │ contact resistance drops sharply.  │
  ├─────────────────────────────┼───────────┼────────────────────────────────────┤
  │ η_act(HER on Ag)            │ ↑ Slight  │ Ag is poor HER catalyst            │
  │                             │           │ (j₀ ~10⁻⁷ A/cm² vs Pt ~10⁻³)     │
  │                             │           │ Partially opposes voltage drop      │
  ├─────────────────────────────┼───────────┼────────────────────────────────────┤
  │ ECSA for HER                │ ↑ Large   │ Flooded 3D GDE structure allows    │
  │                             │           │ HER throughout bulk Ag NP network, │
  │                             │           │ not just triple-phase boundary.    │
  │                             │           │ Cancels kinetic penalty above.     │
  └─────────────────────────────┴───────────┴────────────────────────────────────┘

NET RESULT: E_rev ↓ + η_conc ↓ + iR ↓ all dominate over the weak HER kinetic
penalty on Ag. CELL VOLTAGE DECREASES.

This is the signature of PHYSICAL GDE FLOODING after gas-phase CO2 starvation.
The cell becomes easier to drive at constant current because it has shifted to
a lower-resistance, lower-thermodynamic-barrier reaction — at the cost of
selectivity.

NOTE ON FLUSHING EVENT (~47–50 h, single-serpentine):
  Periodic high-flow CO2 purge physically displaces accumulated water from GDE
  pores, partially re-drying the GDE and restoring CO2RR triple-phase boundary.
  This is why voltage partially recovers and FE(CO) rises briefly after flushing.
  The fact that flushing provides temporary recovery CONFIRMS that GDE flooding
  (not catalyst deactivation) is the dominant failure mode.


================================================================================
3. PARALLEL FLOW-FIELD: VOLTAGE STABLE DESPITE FE(CO) DROP
================================================================================

ROOT FAILURE MECHANISM:
  Water accumulation in low-velocity channels → CO2 channel blockage
  WITHOUT deep GDE pore flooding

KEY DISTINCTION FROM SERPENTINE:
  In parallel channels, low gas velocity allows liquid water droplets to
  accumulate and block the short channel lengths. CO2 access to the GDE
  behind those channels is lost.

  HOWEVER: the Ag NP GDE contains hydrophobic PTFE binder in the carbon
  substrate. This hydrophobicity RESISTS liquid water intrusion into the GDE
  pore bulk from the channel side. Water plugs the channel entrance, but does
  NOT deeply penetrate the GDE pore structure.

WHAT THIS MEANS FOR EACH VOLTAGE TERM:

  • iR (ohmic):         ≈ FLAT. No deep GDE pore flooding → no ionic pathway
                        improvement → ohmic resistance unchanged.
  • η_conc:             ↓ slight. Partial CO2 loss from channel blockage.
  • E_rev:              ↓ −0.11V. Same as serpentine (HER thermodynamics).
  • η_act(HER on Ag):   ↑. Poor Ag HER kinetics, with no significant ECSA gain
                        (GDE not flooded, so limited bulk HER surface available).

NET RESULT: The primary voltage-lowering mechanism (GDE bulk flooding → iR drop)
does NOT occur. The HER kinetic penalty and the E_rev decrease approximately
cancel. CELL VOLTAGE REMAINS STABLE.

SPATIAL AVERAGING INTERPRETATION:
  In parallel, channels fail INDEPENDENTLY (no cascade). At any moment:
    - Open channels:   CO2RR-active, voltage contribution ~3.5V
    - Blocked channels: HER in partially-wet GDE, voltage contribution ~3.3–3.4V
  The measured cell voltage is the spatial average across the full active area.
  As more channels block sequentially, FE(CO) drops — but the voltage average
  shifts only slightly. This produces the observed "stable voltage, falling FE"
  profile.

ADDITIONAL FACTOR — Doubling CO2 flow at ~20 h (observed recovery):
  Temporarily restored FE(CO) from 60% → 80% (first 1–2 h), then collapsed
  again. Interpretation: higher velocity briefly cleared water plugs from
  channels, restoring CO2 access. But since the root cause (low per-channel
  velocity) was unchanged, water re-accumulated within ~3 h and failure resumed.
  This confirms channel-blockage (not GDE flooding) as the parallel failure mode.


================================================================================
4. Ni-SAC SYSTEM CONTRAST: VOLTAGE INCREASES WITH FE(H2) RISE
================================================================================

ROOT FAILURE MECHANISM:
  Catalyst kinetic degradation — Ni single-atom sites deactivate
  (reduction, CO poisoning, loss of atomic dispersion)

FUNDAMENTAL DIFFERENCE:
  There is NO GDE flooding mechanism in Ni-SAC failure. CO2 is physically
  accessible at the electrode. The catalyst simply can no longer use it.

IMPACT ON VOLTAGE TERMS:
  • iR:           ≈ flat (no flooding, no ohmic change)
  • η_conc:       ≈ flat (CO2 still available at electrode surface)
  • E_rev:        ≈ flat (no change in reaction thermodynamics)
  • η_act:        ↑↑ LARGE. As Ni-SAC sites deactivate, more overpotential
                  is demanded to force the same constant current through
                  increasingly poor catalytic sites. HER on residual carbon
                  and deactivated Ni is kinetically slow.

NET RESULT: No voltage-relief mechanisms present. The galvanostat must supply
progressively more voltage to maintain constant current → CELL VOLTAGE INCREASES.

This is the signature of PURE KINETIC DEGRADATION: catalyst failure without
any physical ohmic or mass-transport compensation.

6. DIAGNOSTIC IMPLICATIONS FOR STACK OPERATION
================================================================================

The voltage trend during FE(CO) decline is a LEADING INDICATOR of failure type:

  V DECREASING (serpentine signature):
    → Physical GDE flooding after CO2 starvation underway.
    → Irreversible if allowed to progress. Abort at >0.30V drop.
    → Intervention: restore CO2 access (flushing) and prevent re-flooding
      (higher acid concentration, elevated temperature, higher CO2 flow).

  V STABLE (parallel signature):
    → Water channel blockage without deep GDE flooding.
    → Less catastrophic per event, but each blocked channel is lost until flushed.
    → Intervention: increase CO2 velocity (higher flow rate per channel).
    → Note: increasing total flow rate temporarily helps but doesn't fix
      the root cause (insufficient per-channel velocity in parallel design).

  V INCREASING (kinetic degradation signature):
    → Catalyst deactivation. Not a flow-field or salt problem.
    → Intervention: catalyst regeneration or replacement.
    → Not observed in Ag NP GDE under these conditions.

PRACTICAL ALERT THRESHOLDS FOR 500 cm² STACK MONITORING:
  Warning:    Any single cell ΔV > 0.15V from 1h moving average
  Abort:      Any single cell ΔV > 0.30V from initial steady state
              OR any cell V < 2.5V (possible short or AEM rupture)
              OR any cell V > 4.5V (severe dry-out or catalyst failure)

Goal of 0.5M acid + 50°C intervention:
  → Prevent reaching the GDE flooding stage entirely by dissolving nascent
    salt crystals in situ, keeping CO2 pathways open throughout the run.
    If successful, none of the above failure signatures should appear before 150h.


================================================================================
7. KEY MECHANISTIC INSIGHT (SUMMARY)
================================================================================

Counter-intuitively, the voltage DROP in serpentine represents a cell working
LESS hard electrically — because it has shifted to a lower-resistance, lower-
thermodynamic-barrier reaction (HER in flooded GDE). But it is the most
catastrophic failure from a selectivity and GDE integrity standpoint.

The STABLE voltage in parallel looks "healthier" on the voltage trace, but the
FE(CO) collapse is faster because there is no ohmic or kinetic compensation —
the cell is simply losing active CO2RR area without gaining any electrical
efficiency benefit.

The RISING voltage in Ni-SAC is the most energy-costly failure (galvanostat
works harder and harder), but may be more recoverable if the catalyst can be
regenerated. GDE flooding in the Ag system is typically not self-reversing
without active flushing.

BOTTOM LINE:
  Voltage trend ≠ severity of failure.
  Voltage trend = identity of the failure mechanism.
  Use it to distinguish flooding from degradation from channel blockage —
  and target your intervention accordingly.
